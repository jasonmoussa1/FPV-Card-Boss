Welcome

0 Welcome

What FPV Card Boss does and how this manual is organized.

FPV Card Boss automates the tedious parts of festival FPV: it drives the GoPro Player batch exporter to stabilize your footage, then copies the results to your local, media, and Bella drives, dumps the raws, and advances your card queue — optionally hands-free. A built-in mobile companion lets a pilot or spotter run the shot list and digital slate from a phone, and mark shots done so the editor at the computer knows footage is on the way.

Self-contained

The whole system — the desktop app, the stabilizer robot, the mobile companion web server, and the festival slate — lives inside a single .exe. No installation, no dependencies to chase.

Requirements

1 System Requirements

A short checklist before you start.

ItemRequirement
ComputerWindows 10 or 11, 64-bit. ~250 MB free while running.
GoPro stabilizingGoPro Player (with the Batch Exporter) installed on that computer.
DrivesYour local working drive plus any Media / Bella delivery drives you use.
PhoneAny modern iPhone (Safari) or Android (Chrome). Nothing to install — it runs in the browser.
Off-Wi-Fi phone useTailscale app on the PC and phone (optional — only for cellular / different networks).
Good to know

The phone and computer talk over your local network. On the same Wi-Fi nothing extra is needed. To reach the computer from cellular or a different network, Tailscale creates a secure private link (see Section 6).

What lives where

  • The app and all its tools live inside the .exe.
  • Your settings, calibration, and phone password are saved on the computer that runs the app — so each computer keeps its own.
  • Your phone's shot list is saved on the phone, so it works even with the computer off.

Install

2 Install & First Launch

There is no installer — you just run the file.

  1. Copy the app to the computer. Put FPV Card Boss 1.2.0.exe anywhere — Desktop, a folder, or a USB stick.
  2. Double-click it. It launches directly; the first start takes a few extra seconds as it unpacks.
  3. Clear the SmartScreen warning (first time only). Windows may show a blue "Windows protected your PC" box. Click More info → Run anyway.
Why the warning?

SmartScreen flags any app it hasn't seen widely downloaded yet. This is expected for software you distribute yourself and is safe to run.

Antivirus / firewall

If Windows Firewall asks to allow network access, choose Allow — the app needs it to serve the mobile companion to your phone on the local network.

Setup

3 First-Time Setup

Do this once on each computer. Open Setup in the app.

Step A — Set your folder paths

In Setup, point each path at this computer's folders:

  • Local root — where stabilized footage is organized.
  • Media drive and Bella drive — your delivery destinations.
  • Raw Dump folder — where raw files are consolidated.
  • SD Card drive, GoPro output folder, and the GoPro App path.
Defaults are placeholders

The default paths point at the original computer's folders. Change them to match the machine you're on.

Step B — Calibrate the GoPro Robot

Run 🎯 Calibrate GoPro Robot and follow the 14 on-screen steps — hover each control in GoPro Player and press SPACE to capture its position (including the new Horizon Lock point).

Calibration is per-computer

It is saved for this computer and its exact screen resolution. You must re-calibrate on every new machine or if you change display scaling/resolution.

Step C — Set the phone "Move Files" password

In Setup, type a simple password in Phone "Move Files" Password (shown in plain text so you can read it). This gates only the Move Files section on the phone — the Shot List & Slate stay open to anyone. Leave it blank for no password.

Workflow

4 The Core Workflow

Festival mode, from card in hand to delivered & logged.

  1. ① Create Directory Paths. Select the pilot/card, then click the big green button to create this card's folders.
  2. ② Copy SD Card → Local RAW. The amber button right beneath it copies the SD card into the local RAW folder and verifies the file count.
  3. Set export options. Toggle Horizon Lock on if this batch needs it (Section 5).
  4. 🤖 Auto-Run GoPro Batch. The robot drives GoPro Player to stabilize. Do not touch the mouse or keyboard while it runs.
  5. Move files to STABILIZED. When the export finishes, move the exported clips into the card's STABILIZED folder.
  6. Deliver. Copy to Media Drive, Copy to Bella Drive, and Dump Raws as needed.
  7. Complete Card & Shift. Logs the card to history and advances to the next one.
Do it from the couch

Every one of these delivery steps can also be triggered from your phone's Move Files section (see Section 6).

Automation

5 Auto Mode & Horizon Lock

Hands-free delivery, and the level-horizon export toggle.

Auto Mode (on the computer)

Use the big AUTO / MANUAL button above "Auto-Run GoPro Batch".

  • Manual — you click Move, each delivery, and Complete yourself (the default, deliberate flow).
  • Auto — the moment an export finishes it automatically moves files → Media → Bella → completes the card and advances. If any step fails it stops and alerts you, and never completes a card that didn't fully deliver.
Raws are always manual

Dumping raws is never part of Auto mode (or the phone's Send to All) — it is always a deliberate click on the Dump Raws button.

Auto is great for walking away

Turn it on when you trust the setup and need to step away. A live status line shows each step as it runs.

Horizon Lock

Toggle Horizon Lock ON (it turns blue 🌐) before running a batch. The robot will enable Horizon Lock in the GoPro batch exporter so your footage exports level. Turn it OFF for a normal export.

Needs its calibration point

If you turn it on but haven't captured the Horizon Lock position during calibration, the app warns you and simply skips the click — re-calibrate to enable it.

Mobile

6 The Mobile Companion

Run the shot list, slate, and deliveries from your phone.

Connecting — scan a QR code

  1. Open the panel. In the computer's Setup, find "Open on your phone" — each address shows a QR code and a Copy button (tap the ? beside it for an in-app reminder).
  2. Scan with the phone camera (or Copy the link and paste it into the phone browser).
  3. Add to Home Screen. In the browser's Share menu choose Add to Home Screen for an app icon, full screen, and saved data.

Which address? (they're different on purpose)

AddressUse it for
★ Tailscale HTTPS
https://…ts.net		The recommended one. Works anywhere with Tailscale on. The only address that opens offline (computer off) and enables the slate microphone. Add this to the Home Screen.
Tailscale HTTP
100.x.x.x		Works anywhere with Tailscale on, but online-only — no offline, no mic.
Same Wi-Fi (LAN)
192.168.x.x		Quick access on the same Wi-Fi as the computer. Online-only.
Why HTTPS?

Browsers only allow a web app to work offline or use the microphone over a secure (https://) address — that's the Tailscale HTTPS one. Open it once while the computer is running so the phone saves a copy; after that it opens with the computer off.

Shot List

7 Shot List on the Phone

Build it, import it, mark it — and keep the computer in sync.

Three ways to add shots

  • ➕ Add Shot — enter Artist, Stage, Festival (plus optional Pilot/Day) and keep adding more in a row.
  • 📄 Add CSV — paste rows or upload a .csv/.txt. Columns: Artist, Stage, Festival, Pilot, Day.
  • ⤵ Import PC — pull the computer's loaded shot list onto the phone (each row links 1:1 to the computer).

Marking shots — and the red light

Each shot has Mark Done, Skip, ✏️ Edit, and 🎬 Slate. For shots imported from the computer, marking Done or Skipped updates the same row on the computer and triggers a red blinking light + ding there — so the editor knows that artist's footage is completed (or skipped) and incoming.

Works off-network too

If you mark a shot while briefly off Wi-Fi or before Tailscale reconnects, it's queued and retried automatically — it reaches the computer the moment you're back. A "⏳ waiting to sync" note shows while anything is pending.

It remembers you

Your selected pilot and day are saved on the phone — back out, open the slate, or close the app, and you return to your pilot/day with no re-selecting. The shot list also opens with the computer off.

Resetting on the computer (📋 Shot List window)

In the computer's 📋 Shot List window, a finished day shows ↩ Reopen Day at the top and bottom of that day — it sends every shot for that pilot/day (completed and skipped) back to pending. ⟳ Reset Entire List (top toolbar) does the same for every pilot and day at once. A shot you Skip in the list is also skipped by the card workflow, so it won't reappear as the next card.

Slate

8 The Festival Slate

A high-contrast digital slate with GoPro time-sync.

Open it from any shot (🎬 Slate, pre-filled with that artist/stage/pilot) or from Simple Slate on the home screen. Use the ‹ Back button to return to where you came from.

Features

  • GoPro Labs sync QR — scan it with a GoPro to sync its clock/timecode. Modes: Stable, Med, Fast, and Freeze (a frozen snapshot to stop screen flicker). Live TC for 24/25/30/60 fps.
  • Take counter — bump takes on the linked shot; the count saves back and the slate remembers it next time.
  • Themes — Vibrant, Aurora, Sunrise, Lime, Laser neon profiles.
  • Landscape lock & fullscreen — a rotate-to-landscape guard and a clean fullscreen slate for the camera.
Mic & screen-wake need the HTTPS address

Audio take-recording and keeping the screen awake only work when you open the app via the ★ Tailscale HTTPS address (Section 6). Over a plain http:// address browsers block them. Everything else — QR sync, the slate, takes, and shot sync — works on any address.

9 Tips & Troubleshooting

Quick answers to common situations.

SituationWhat to do
Phone says "Offline" / can't reach the computerMake sure the app is open on the PC and you're on the same Wi-Fi (or Tailscale is on and you used the 100.x address). The Shot List & Slate still work offline.
Marking a shot doesn't update the computerThat shot must be linked — tap ⤵ Import PC once; it links your existing shots 1:1 to the computer. Then Done/Skip will sync and light up.
The phone shows an old screen after an updateReload the page; the build stamp at the very bottom should match the latest. On an installed (home-screen) app, close and reopen it.
Robot clicks the wrong spotsRe-run Calibrate GoPro Robot — calibration is tied to this computer's resolution/scaling.
Mic recording in the slate won't startExpected over a plain address — it needs HTTPS. Use the rest of the slate normally.
Moving the app to a new computerJust copy the .exe. Then redo Setup: paths, calibration, and password are per-computer.
Find the build

The mobile page shows its build at the very bottom (e.g. build pwa-2026-06-02-x-offline-first). Handy when confirming an update reached the phone.
