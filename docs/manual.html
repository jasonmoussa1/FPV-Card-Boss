Welcome

0 Welcome

What this is, who it's for, and how to read it.

FPV Card Boss automates the punishing parts of festival FPV: it drives the GoPro Player batch exporter to stabilize your footage with identical settings every time, then copies the results to your local, media, and Bella drives, dumps the raws, and advances your card queue — optionally hands-free. A built-in mobile companion lets a pilot or spotter run the shot list and digital slate from a phone and mark shots done so the editor at the computer knows footage is on the way.

Two ways to read this

New to the job? Read straight through — Section 1 explains the work itself before the buttons. Done it before and just need a refresher? Jump to the Quick Reference on the second-to-last page.

Now cross-platform

Everything in this manual works the same on a Windows PC and a Mac. Where something differs under the hood, it's called out. The app asks which computer it's on the first time you launch it.

The Job

1 The Job & The SOP

Read this first if you've never wrangled cards at a festival.

You are the stabilizer operator for an FPV drone team at a music festival. Pilots fly all night and hand you SD cards — often at 3 AM, out of order, several at once. Your job: get every card's footage copied, stabilized, and delivered to the right places before the editors need it, and logged so nothing is lost.

The one rule that never breaks

Never destroy the originals. Footage always lives in two halves: RAW — the untouched clips straight off the card — and STABILIZED — the smoothed exports. They live in separate subfolders for every shot, so originals are never overwritten and you can always re-export from RAW if anything goes wrong.

Why stabilize, and why a robot

FPV drones are tiny and twitchy; raw footage is unusably shaky. GoPro Player's HyperSmooth Pro (ReelSteady) flattens that motion. Every clip from every pilot must use the same settings so footage cuts together cleanly in the edit — that consistency is the whole reason this app exists. The app physically drives GoPro Player (a "robot") so the settings are identical every time and you're not hand-clicking sliders half-asleep.

Where footage goes

Local — your fast working drive. Media Drive — the master archive (RAW + STABILIZED together, named by card ID). Bella / Social — stabilized clips only, named by artist (the social team must never get raws). Then the card is logged in the shared Media Master sheet.

The whole loop in one breath

Card in hand → make folders → copy card to RAW → robot stabilizes → move exports to STABILIZED → copy to Media + Bella → log it → wipe the card → next.

2 Requirements, Install & Platform

What you need, and telling the app which computer it's on.

You'll need

ComputerA Windows 10/11 PC or a Mac (Apple Silicon or Intel, macOS 10.14+). A GPU that decodes HEVC.
GoPro PlayerGoPro Player + HyperSmooth Pro (ReelSteady) installed, with the $99 HyperSmooth Pro unlock.
DrivesA fast local drive, plus the Media and Bella/Social drives (or network volumes).
PhoneiPhone or Android for the mobile companion (optional but recommended). Tailscale app for field use.

Choosing your platform

The first time you launch, the app asks "Which computer is this — Windows PC or Mac?" and pre-selects what it detects. Your choice decides which engine drives GoPro Player and your drives. You can change it any time from the small platform badge in the corner.

Mac: two one-time permissions

On a Mac, the first time the robot runs, macOS asks for Accessibility (to move the mouse/keyboard) and Screen Recording (so calibration can read the screen). Approve both in System Settings ▸ Privacy & Security. This is the Mac equivalent of the Windows firewall prompt — one time per machine.

What differs between Windows & Mac

Nothing you can see: same workflow, buttons, settings, slate, and companion. Behind the scenes, file copying uses Robocopy on Windows and rsync on Mac, the robot uses a different click engine, and GoPro exports land in Videos on Windows vs ~/Movies on Mac. Calibration is saved separately per computer.

Setup

3 First-Time Setup

Do this once on each computer. Open Setup.

  1. Set the folder paths. Point each at this computer's locations: Local root, Media drive, Bella drive, Raw Dump folder, SD Card drive/volume, and the GoPro output folder.
  2. Add your pilots. Each pilot gets a card prefix and starting number — e.g. L from 1 produces L_001, L_002… The active pilot sets the next card ID and filters the shot list.
  3. Calibrate the GoPro robot. Run 🎯 Calibrate GoPro Robot and follow the 14 steps — hover each control in GoPro Player and press SPACE (Section 5).
  4. Set the phone "Move Files" password (optional). It gates only the Move Files section on the phone; the Shot List & Slate stay open. Leave blank for none.
Calibration is per-computer & per-resolution

It's saved for this machine at its exact screen resolution. Re-calibrate on a new computer, if you change display scaling/resolution, or if you move/resize the GoPro window — otherwise the robot clicks the wrong spots. Windows and Mac calibrations are stored independently.

Folder structure the app builds

Local: [Local]\[Event]\[Pilot]\Day[N]\[Artist]\RAW + \STABILIZED Media: [Media]\[CardID]\ (RAW + STABILIZED together) Bella: [Bella]\[Artist]\ (STABILIZED clips only) Dual: ...\STABILIZED\HORIZON LOCK\ (2nd pass, if Dual Mode)

Workflow

4 The Core Workflow

Festival mode — from card in hand to delivered & logged.

  1. Pick the card's assignment. Confirm the active pilot and day, then pick the artist/shot (use Choose From List when cards arrive out of order). This sets the card ID and folder names.
  2. Create Directory Paths. The big green button makes this card's RAW & STABILIZED folders locally, on the Media Drive, and the artist folder on Bella.
  3. Copy SD Card → Local RAW. Copies the card in and verifies file count and size. If RAW already has files, it copies into a fresh BATCH_02 subfolder so the robot only stabilizes the new clips.
  4. Set export options. Toggle Horizon Lock or Dual Mode if this batch needs them (Section 6).
  5. 🤖 Auto-Run GoPro Batch. The robot drives GoPro Player. Hands off the mouse and keyboard until it finishes.
  6. Move Files → STABILIZED. When the export finishes, move the new clips into the card's STABILIZED folder (only files created after the robot started are taken).
  7. Deliver. Copy to Media Drive, Copy to Bella, and Dump Raws as needed.
  8. Complete Card. Logs the card, advances the number, clears for the next. Then update the Media Master sheet.
From the couch

Every step from "Move Files" on can be triggered from your phone, or run hands-free with Auto mode (Section 6).

5 Stabilization & Calibration

The locked-in look, and teaching the robot your screen.

The SOP export settings — every clip, every pilot

SettingValue
CodecHEVC (H.265) 10-bit
HyperSmooth ProON
Smoothness15
Cropping15
Aspect Ratio8:7 (widest field of view)
Why "un-gain" matters

Smoothness and Cropping are linked by default — moving one drags the other. The robot first clicks the un-gain / chain-link button to unlink them, so it can set each to 15 independently. That's why it's a calibration point.

Calibration — 14 points

GoPro Player can't be driven normally, so the app moves the mouse to fixed positions. Hover each control and press SPACE in this order:

  • batch list · 10-bit · HyperSmooth · un-gain · Horizon Lock
  • Smoothness start & end · Cropping start & end
  • aspect-ratio open · 8:7 option · drop zone · Start · Remove
Re-calibrate when the window moves

The robot clicks fixed screen positions. Move/resize the GoPro window, change resolution, or switch computers → re-calibrate or it misses.

Modes

6 Auto · Horizon Lock · Dual Mode

Hands-free delivery, a level horizon, and both versions at once.

Auto vs Manual

  • Manual (default) — you click Move, each delivery, and Complete yourself.
  • Auto — when an export finishes it auto-moves → Media → Bella → completes the card and advances. If any step fails it stops and alerts you, and never completes a card that didn't fully deliver.
Raws are always manual

Dumping raws is never part of Auto mode or the phone's "Send to All" — it's a deliberate click, unless you enable Auto-Dump in Setup. Always dump raws before wiping a card.

Horizon Lock

Toggle it ON (turns blue 🌐) before running a batch and the robot enables Horizon Lock in the exporter so footage exports level. If it's on but not calibrated, the app warns and skips it — re-calibrate to enable.

Dual Mode — both versions in one run

Turn on Dual Mode and the robot exports every clip twice: a normal pass into STABILIZED, then a second pass with Horizon Lock into a STABILIZED\HORIZON LOCK subfolder. Editors get the regular and the level-horizon version with no filename collisions. It takes about twice as long and needs the Horizon Lock calibration point.

When to use Dual

Use it when the team hasn't decided between locked or unlocked horizon for a set — deliver both and let them choose in the edit.

Mobile

7 The Mobile Companion

Run the shot list, slate, and deliveries from your phone.

Connecting — three addresses

In Setup, under "Open on your phone", scan a QR code or tap Copy. Then use Add to Home Screen for a full-screen app.

  • ★ Tailscale (HTTPS) — https://…ts.net. The best one: works anywhere with Tailscale on, opens Shot List & Slate even with the computer OFF, and enables the slate mic.
  • Tailscale (HTTP) — works anywhere with Tailscale on, but online-only.
  • Same Wi-Fi (LAN, 192.168…) — quick access on the same Wi-Fi. Online-only.
Why HTTPS is special

Browsers only allow offline caching and microphone access over a secure (HTTPS) address — the Tailscale HTTPS link. Open it once while the computer is running (Tailscale on) so the phone saves a copy; after that it works offline.

Field Tools

8 Shot List, Slate & Site Map

Plan the night, slate the shots, find your way around.

The shot list

Fill it three ways: Load Sample EDC (practice), Import CSV (the production's real list — drag a .csv onto the window; columns Artist, Stage, Festival, Pilot, Day), or add by hand. Each shot is pending, completed, or skipped. Marking Done/Skip on the phone updates the same row on the computer and fires a red blink + ding there, so the editor knows footage is incoming. Skipped shots drop out of the "next card" queue. Off-network changes queue and retry automatically.

Live auto-sync — no import step

The phone keeps the shot list in sync with the computer automatically. Change the list on the PC and connected phones update within a few seconds. Anything already marked completed or skipped stays that way — only new or future shots change.

The festival slate

  • GoPro Labs sync QR — scan with a GoPro to sync its clock/timecode (Stable, Med, Fast, Freeze; live TC for 24/25/30/60 fps).
  • Take counter — bumps save back to the linked shot.
  • Themes, landscape lock, and a clean fullscreen slate for the camera.
Mic & screen-wake need HTTPS

Audio take-recording and keeping the screen awake only work over the Tailscale HTTPS address. Plain http:// blocks them.

Site map

In Setup ▸ Mobile Dashboard, add a venue map image (PNG/JPG/WEBP/GIF/BMP/SVG). It shows full-screen on the phone, works offline once cached, and updates on every phone instantly when you replace it.

9 Simple Mode & Troubleshooting

The lighter flow, and quick fixes.

Simple Mode

Switch to Simple mode for one-off or non-assignment work. Instead of picking an artist from a shot list, you type a folder name; the app makes RAW/STABILIZED under Local\[Show]\[Folder], runs the same SD-copy and robot, and copies to Media/Bella by toggle. No shot-list queue, no Dump Raws, no Complete-and-advance — the fast path for simple jobs.

Troubleshooting

SymptomFix
Robot clicks the wrong spotsRe-run Calibrate GoPro Robot (tied to this computer's resolution/scaling and the GoPro window position).
Mac robot does nothing / errorsGrant Accessibility + Screen Recording in System Settings ▸ Privacy & Security, then re-run.

New computerCopy the app, redo Setup — paths, calibration & password are per-computer.
